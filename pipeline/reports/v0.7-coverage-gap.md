## Categories the LLM verification pass should close

Ordered by estimated impact on closing the 1.5 pp gap:

1. **Cost reduction** (~30 cards): "this spell costs {N} less to cast", "the next instant or sorcery you cast costs {1} less". A dedicated `effect.cost_reduction` rule could catch most. Edge: spellslinger payoff.

2. **Impulse draw / exile-and-cast** (~25 cards): "Exile the top N cards. You may cast one of them this turn." Modern mechanic across many sets. Worth `effect.impulse_draw` paired with `condition.cares_graveyard` (the exiled cards aren't in graveyard but the mechanic is library-manipulation-adjacent).

3. **Sacrifice as activated-ability cost** (~20 cards): "{T}, Sacrifice this creature: <effect>". Aristocrats enabler. Distinct from `effect.sacrifice_permanent` (which is "Sacrifice a creature: <effect>" — targeting any creature). The "Sacrifice this" cost is its own pattern.

4. **Land-fetch (basic land tutor)** (~15 cards): "Search your library for a basic land card, put it onto the battlefield tapped." Existing `effect.tutors_subtype.*` doesn't include basic lands; ramp/fixing archetype.

5. **`can't be blocked` evasion variants** (~22 cards): "This creature can't be blocked except by creatures with reach", etc. Slice 3 territory in the original brief — defer.

6. **"Becomes a copy of" creature mutation** — handled by Family 7's `effect.copy_permanent` expansion. Should already be caught; verify after the LLM pass.

## What the LLM should NOT do

- **Don't broaden** existing tagged rules (e.g., don't loosen `effect.destroy_creature` to catch edge cases — those are intentionally tight).
- **Don't tag** cards that legitimately have no mechanical interaction (e.g., vanilla creatures with just a single keyword that Family 11 also missed because the keyword is non-evergreen). Those will continue to drag the denominator down; that's correct.

## Verification approach for the LLM pass

For each candidate untagged card:
1. Read the normalized oracle text.
2. Determine the dominant mechanic (or "no mechanic" — vanilla / land / unique).
3. If the mechanic matches an *existing* v0.7 tag, file a regex-broadening recommendation (with the missed phrase).
4. If the mechanic is *new* (e.g., cost reduction), recommend a v0.8 family.
5. Track precision/recall on a held-out sample of 100 known-correctly-tagged cards before applying any changes.
